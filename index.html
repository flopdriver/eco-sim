About This Ecosystem

This is a true pixel-based ecosystem simulation where every element is simulated at the individual pixel level. Unlike sprite-based simulations, everything here emerges naturally from simple rules and interactions between pixels, creating complex emergent behaviors.

Plants

Plants grow from seeds, developing roots to absorb water and nutrients, stems that reach for light, and leaves that perform photosynthesis. They produce seeds to reproduce when conditions are favorable.

Water

Water flows realistically with gravity, can be absorbed by soil, and is transported through plants for growth. It evaporates in warm conditions and returns as rain, completing the water cycle.

Soil

Soil holds moisture and nutrients. It can be dry, wet, or fertile, affecting plant growth and ecosystem health. Dead organisms decompose into soil, returning nutrients to the system.

Insects

Insects move around, seeking plants to eat. They reproduce when they have enough energy and die when they don't. Their population naturally balances with the available plant life.

Worms

Worms move through soil, eating dead matter and aerating the soil in the process. They improve soil fertility and help complete the decomposition cycle of the ecosystem.

Weather

The simulation includes a day/night cycle, rain events, and temperature fluctuations that affect all organisms. These environmental factors create dynamic conditions that the ecosystem must adapt to.
